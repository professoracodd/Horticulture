Plant Nutrient
Terminology Visual Guide

A pictorial reference for identifying deficiency and toxicity symptoms · A. Codd

🔍 Visual Symptom Terms
Symptom
Chlorosis
Yellowing of plant tissue caused by loss of chlorophyll. The leaf turns pale green, yellow, or nearly white.
Symptom
Interveinal Chlorosis
Yellowing occurs between the leaf veins, while the veins themselves remain green. Creates a distinctive striped or netted appearance.
Symptom
Necrosis / Necrotic Spotting
Areas of dead, brown or tan tissue on the leaf. Can appear as scattered spots, blotches, or lesions where cells have died.
Symptom
Scorched / Burned Margins
Leaf edges appear dry, brown, and burnt. Often associated with potassium or chloride deficiency causing a "tip burn" effect along margins.
Symptom
Cupped / Rolled Leaves
Leaves curl inward (cupped) or roll along their length. A response to stress that can indicate molybdenum or nitrogen deficiency.
Pattern
Abrupt Boundary
A sharp, well-defined line between dead and living tissue on a leaf. This clear demarcation (seen in Cl deficiency) distinguishes it from gradual yellowing.
Pattern
Generalized vs. Localized
Generalized: symptoms spread evenly across the whole plant or leaf. Localized: symptoms appear in specific areas only, like spots or patches.
Symptom
Necrotic Lesions
Defined areas of dead, collapsed tissue, larger or more distinct than simple spots. Often tan, brown, or gray with a surrounding halo of discoloration.
🌱 Leaf Position Terms

Mobile nutrients deficiency appears in older/lower leaves first (plant moves nutrients upward). Immobile nutrients deficiency appears in newer/younger leaves first.

🔄 Nutrient Mobility
🟢 Mobile Nutrients
These nutrients can move freely within the plant. When deficient, the plant relocates them from older leaves to newer growth — so deficiency symptoms appear in older, lower leaves first.
N – Nitrogen P – Phosphorus K – Potassium Mg – Magnesium Mo – Molybdenum Cl – Chloride
🟠 Immobile Nutrients
These nutrients cannot be relocated once deposited in a leaf. Deficiency symptoms first appear in younger, newer growth at the top of the plant.
Fe – Iron Mn – Manganese Zn – Zinc B – Boron Cu – Copper Ca – Calcium S – Sulfur Ni – Nickel

Immobile · Micronutrient

B

Immobile · Micronutrient
Terminal bud: leaves twisted, brittle, light green at base
Cu
Copper
Immobile · Micronutrient
Young leaves withered at tips; will eventually die
Mo

Mobile · Micronutrient
Pale leaves scorched, cupped, or rolled; necrotic spotting
Cl
Chloride
Mobile · Micronutrient
Distinct lesions with abrupt boundary between dead and live tissue
Ni
Nickel
Immobile · Micronutrient
Recently added essential nutrient; chlorosis and interveinal chlorosis in younger leaves
