01 — Core Concept

What is Staging?

Instead of switching all equipment on or off at once, a staging system activates cooling and heating in progressive steps — responding only with what's needed, when it's needed.

01
Normal (N)

The baseline state. HAF circulation fans run continuously. No active heating or cooling engaged.

C1–C6
Cooling Stages

Graduated cooling from mild (C1: louvers open, light fan) to aggressive (C5–6: all fans + wet pads running).

H1–H2
Heating Stages

H1 provides mild heat. H2 engages more aggressive heating. Propane or natural gas heaters with convection tubes.

↓↓
Why It Matters

Prevents temperature overshoot, reduces equipment wear, and significantly lowers energy costs year-round.

02 — Energy Impact

Staging Saves Energy

Without staging, equipment switches fully on and off, creating temperature swings. With staging, the system responds proportionally — no overshooting, smaller swings.

⚠ Without Staging
High Energy Use
  • All equipment turns on simultaneously
  • Large temperature swings above and below setpoint
  • Repeated overcorrection cycles
  • High electrical load spikes
✓ With Staging
Low Energy Use
  • Equipment activates in incremental stages
  • Temperature stays in the target zone
  • No overshooting the setpoint
  • Only what's needed, when it's needed

04 — Equipment

Greenhouse Equipment

Understanding each piece of equipment helps configure staging correctly for your specific house layout.

🌬 HAF Fans

Horizontal Air Flow fans run in a racetrack pattern to even out hot and cold spots. Set to "N" (normally on) — they run continuously in the normal stage.

🔥 Heaters

Propane or natural gas unit heaters. Some systems use convection tubes (jet tubes) for more uniform heat distribution across the house.

💧 Evap Pads

Wet pads combined with exhaust fans for evaporative cooling. Most effective in hot, dry conditions. Should activate only after louvers and fans are already running.

🪟 Louvers

Intake vents that should open independently from the pad pump. When wired together, you lose control over humidity management.

🏠 Ridge Vents

Optional passive or active vents at the peak of the greenhouse. Allow hot air to escape naturally — useful as a first-stage cooling response before fans engage.

☂️ Shade Curtains

Automatic curtains reduce solar heat gain, cutting cooling load during peak sun hours. An optional but valuable addition for summer management.

Wiring Problem Identified The pad pump and louvers are wired to the same relay — they always activate together. This prevents separating the cooling stages: in winter, air exchanges always route through the wet pads, adding moisture and increasing fungal pressure even when only ventilation is needed.
⚠ Current Setup — Winter
Humidity Problem
  • Humidity is already high in winter
  • Every air exchange routes through wet pads
  • Adds moisture unnecessarily
  • Increased fungal disease pressure
✓ Better Design (If Rewired)
Separated Channels
  • C1: Louvers + Fan 1 (dry ventilation)
  • C2: + Fan 2 (more airflow, still dry)
  • C3–C6: Pads ON (only when truly hot)
  • Works correctly in both summer and winter

Solution: Channel 4 is currently empty and should be rewired to control the louvers independently. This gives the controller the ability to open vents for ventilation (C1) without activating the pad pump — critical for proper humidity management in cooler months.

06 — Advanced Control

DIF & Temperature Drop

Staging works alongside setpoints and ramping to give growers precise control over plant growth characteristics.

DIF Calculator

+13
Positive DIF
Plants will elongate and stretch more. Warm days and cool nights promote stem extension.
Setpoints

Three daily periods: Night → DROP → Day. Each has its own low and high setpoint range that tells the controller when to engage heating or cooling stages.

Ramping

Controls how quickly the setpoint transitions between periods. A slow ramp avoids shocking plants; a fast ramp is used for DROP before sunrise.

Temperature DROP

A brief cool period starting before sunrise, held for a few hours after. Creates a negative DIF effect to reduce stretch without keeping nights cold all the time.
